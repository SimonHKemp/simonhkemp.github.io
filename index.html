Projects

Undergraduate Hall Effect Thruster

Role: Instrumentation
An undergraduate, cross-institutional project to design, build, and test a Hall effect thruster. My role was to design and build a low cost test stand capable of accurately measuring the low output force of the thruster in a vacuum. The results were presented at the 2022 International Electric Propulsion Conference in Boston MA and are published in the Journal of Electric Propulsion.

Olin Rocketry

Role: Payload & Recovery Lead
Project team developing rockets to compete in the Intercollegiate Rocket Engineering Competition (IREC), a competition where teams from around the world design and build high powered model rockets to reach a target altitude of 10,000. In my second year on the team I was the recovery sub-team lead, developing a pneumatic release system; following I became payload lead, developing a 3U CubeSat that could be deployed during descent.

S.A.L.L.E

Role: Technical Lead
A cart with an integrated elevator developed for Blue Origin that can lift and transport 100 lbs of avionics equipment. As technical lead my role was to manage the design, construction, and verify structural integrity using FEA.

Sidewinder

Role: Composites & Mechanical
A student built, low cost, Carbon fiber filament winder, made to manufacture Composite tubes and measure the relationship between the winding angle of a carbon fiber (CF) tube and the flexural load it can support.

SparrowHawk

Role: Simulation and Analysis
An ongoing project to build a drone with variable sweep wings. The goal is to create a drone that will dynamically adjust the wing position in flight to optimize efficiency. If successful this will allow for longer flight times, and a multipurpose drone with good high and low speed flight characteristics.

Beest

Role: Mechanical
A walking robot inspired by Theo Jansen's Strandbeest sculpture. We created a small-scale Strandbeest powered by electric motors that can identify a person and follow them using computer vision. My role was to design and build the legs based on Jansen's Linkage, and create a unidirectional-high-friction foot for optimized walking on smooth surfaces.

Great Basin National Park

National Park Ranger Intern

Great Basin National park is home to Lehman caves, a cave system with 2 miles of mapped passages filled with cave formations. As a Ranger I developed and guided tours of the cave, combining history, cave science, and conservation while trying to inspire awe. I also assisted with park research on project's from cave mapping to Biology surveys.

Great Basin Volunteer

Volunteer

In addition to my time as a ranger I also volunteer for the annual lint camp. This involves leading groups of volunteers in cave restoration, uncovering and cleaning formations that had been buried for over 100 years. As well as removing lint and dust unintentionally left by the thousands of park visitors who tour the cave each year. My first time volunteering our work was featured in the LA Times
